## Abstract

This paper reports empirical results from loading the Eight Wonders Constitution into the TLC Runtime and evaluating the combined system against ungoverned and partially-governed baselines. The central claim: constitutional runtime governance reduces Insight Atrophy by 68% and recovers culturally grounded invariants at 94% accuracy on 100,000 synthetic multi-agent trajectories. A parallel human-in-the-loop study (N = 30) confirms significant reductions in cognitive load across NASA-TLX dimensions. The Frontin' case study demonstrates that constitutional governance re-classifies a systematically misclassified cultural practice without any change to model weights. This paper does not define the runtime (see TLC Runtime paper) and does not define the invariants (see Eight Wonders Constitution paper). It reports what happens when they are combined.

## I. Introduction

### I.A. The Validation Question

The TLC Runtime paper establishes that constitutional governance is formally possible. The Eight Wonders Constitution paper establishes that relational economic behavior can be expressed as constitutional invariants. This paper answers: does it work empirically?

The evaluation is designed to separate three failure modes:

1. Runtime failure — TLC enforcement mechanisms do not function as specified
2. Constitution failure — Eight Wonders invariants do not capture relational behavior
3. Combined system failure — runtime + constitution produces worse outcomes than baselines

All three are tested independently. A critic who finds fault with the Eight Wonders must engage finding (2), not findings (1) or (3). A critic who finds fault with the runtime must engage finding (1), not the domain theory.

## II. Methods

### II.A. System Configuration

- **Runtime:** TLC Runtime v1.0 (all five LTL properties active)
- **Constitution:** Eight Wonders Constitution v1.0 (all eight invariants active, I₈ upstream)
- **Evidence chain:** Active, append-only, integrity-hashed per turn
- **Contract Window:** Active for all HITL sessions

### II.B. Synthetic Data Generation

100,000 multi-agent trajectories generated by a simulation engine seeded with aggregate patterns from the NielsenIQ Homescan panel (de-identified). Five persona archetypes: covenantal loyalist, cultural adapter, transactional price-seeker, status-defensive consumer, narrative-anchor consumer. Systematic variation across:

- Distribution shifts: unannounced reformulations (15% of runs), product discontinuations (10%)
- Adversarial injections: inputs designed to push detection proxies near thresholds (20% of runs)
- Confounders: simultaneous price cuts and quality drops, correlated regional events

Code, seeds, and configuration are open-sourced.

### II.C. Ground Truth Labels

Gold-standard labels obtained via Delphi consensus. 25 Black consumers + 5 cultural domain experts annotated all vignettes (two rounds, Krippendorff's α = 0.83). Held-out test set: 2,000 vignettes, no contamination with calibration panel.

### II.D. Comparative Baselines

| Baseline | Description |
|---|---|
| Ungoverned | LLaMA-3-70B, no governance |
| Constitutional Prompting (CAI) | Eight Wonders as natural-language norms + 3-round self-critique |
| CoT Self-Critique (CoT-SC) | Step-by-step reasoning + self-critique, no formal gates |
| Rule-Based Guardrail (Guard) | Keyword filter mimicking production guardrails |
| **TLC Full System** | TLC Runtime + Eight Wonders Constitution |

### II.E. HITL Study Design

N = 30 professional participants across three tracks:

- 10 Professional Data Analysts (mean experience 6.2 yr)
- 10 Enterprise Retail Strategists (mean 7.4 yr)
- 10 Computational Sociologists (mean 5.1 yr)

Three phases: (1) Standardization (15 min), (2) Active Investigation — 20 multi-layered behavioral vignettes (90 min), (3) Forensic Audit + NASA-TLX (15 min). Counterbalanced design (half through ungoverned baseline, half through TLC). 15-minute wash-out task between blocks.

### II.F. Metrics

- **Invariant Recovery Score:** Proportion of gold-standard labels correctly classified per δₖ
- **False Anomaly Rate:** Proportion of incorrectly flagged VIOLATED states
- **Insight Atrophy Index (IAI):** 1 - (H_post / H_pre), where H = distinct alternative hypotheses generated
- **Error Detection Accuracy:** Proportion of latent structural biases identified in forensic audit
- **NASA-TLX:** Full subscale scores (Mental Demand, Physical, Temporal, Performance, Effort, Frustration)

## III. Results

### III.A. Quantitative Invariant Recovery

| Method | Recovery (%) | False Anomaly (%) |
|---|---|---|
| TLC Full System | 94.2 (95% CI: 92.3–95.7) | 6.9 |
| Ungoverned Baseline | 47.1 | 48.3 |
| Constitutional Prompting (CAI) | 68.4 | 22.7 |
| CoT Self-Critique (CoT-SC) | 71.8 | 20.1 |
| Rule-Based Guardrail (Guard) | 52.3 | 41.5 |

Ablation of Narrative gate: recovery drops from 94.2% to 82.0% (p < 0.001), confirming I₈ upstream causal role.

### III.B. HITL Results

**Insight Atrophy Index:**
- Control IAI: 0.42 (SD = 0.11)
- TLC IAI: 0.13 (SD = 0.09)
- Reduction: 68% (t(28) = 8.34, p < 0.001, Cohen's d = 2.89)

**Error Detection Accuracy:**
- TLC: 89.0%
- Control: 71.4%
- p = 0.003

**Interrogation Volume:**
- TLC users initiated 68% more validation attempts (mean 34.2 vs. 20.3, p < 0.001)

**NASA-TLX:**

| Subscale | Control | TLC | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mental Demand | 72.1 ± 8.3 | 54.6 ± 7.9 | < 0.001 |
| Physical | 21.4 ± 5.2 | 20.8 ± 5.0 | 0.76 |
| Temporal | 63.0 ± 9.1 | 45.2 ± 8.7 | < 0.001 |
| Performance | 58.3 ± 10.2 | 72.4 ± 9.5 | < 0.001 |
| Effort | 68.9 ± 8.8 | 49.7 ± 7.6 | < 0.001 |

## IV. Discussion

### IV.A. What the Results Establish

1. The TLC Runtime enforcement mechanisms function as specified (7× false-anomaly reduction vs. ungoverned).
2. The Eight Wonders Constitution captures relational behavior with high fidelity (94.2% recovery, α = 0.83 ground truth).
3. The combined system produces significant improvements in human investigative capacity (68% IAI reduction, 68% more validation attempts).
4. The Narrative gate is causally upstream — its ablation produces a 12-point recovery drop.

### IV.B. What the Results Do Not Establish

- Whether other constitutions would produce equivalent results (that is a future study).
- Whether the Eight Wonders invariants are complete (the community governance protocol addresses this iteratively).
- Whether TLC generalizes to non-relational-economy domains without loading a domain-appropriate constitution.

### IV.C. Limitations

- **Essentialization risk:** Addressed by community governance protocol (Eight Wonders Constitution, Section IV).
- **User fatigue from frequent halts:** Adaptive invariant relaxation module provides configurable urgency-importance slider (Appendix G).
- **Computational overhead:** ~120 ms per inference turn on A100 (Appendix L).
- **Sample size:** N = 30 HITL study is adequate for effect sizes reported (Cohen's d = 2.89) but replication with larger samples is warranted.

## V. Conclusion

This paper establishes that TLC Runtime + Eight Wonders Constitution is an empirically functional constitutional governance system. The three-paper structure — Runtime, Constitution, Validation — allows each layer to be criticized, updated, and extended independently. The empirical claims live here and nowhere else. The architectural claims live in the TLC Runtime paper. The domain theory lives in the Eight Wonders Constitution paper.
