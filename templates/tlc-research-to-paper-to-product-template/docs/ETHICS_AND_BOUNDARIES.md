## Scope of this document

for the {{PROJECT_NAME}} research project.

---

## Content boundaries

### What may be captured

- Researcher's own observable behaviors (time, actions, ratings, notes)
- Researcher's own cognitive state observations (self-report)
- Metadata about sessions (duration, date, platform, construct scores)
- Post-session observations written in the researcher's own words

### What must never be captured in committed files

- Full text of copyrighted course materials (lessons, assessments, quizzes)
- Quiz answers or assessment answer banks
- Platform-exported LMS data
- Screenshots containing copyrighted content (store in evidence/screenshots/ which is gitignored)
- Any PII from other participants or users of the platform
- Any material that the platform's terms of service prohibit from being reproduced

---

## Participant boundary

This study has one participant: the researcher.

No other individuals are studied, observed, quoted, or identified in any
committed artifact unless they have given explicit written consent and
that consent is documented here.

---

## AI tool use boundary

AI tools (including Hermes) may be used for:
- Data transfer (workbook writes, format conversion)
- Analysis scripts
- Report generation

AI tools must not be used to:
- Reconstruct or estimate missing session data
- Generate synthetic observations
- Produce paper section content that would be presented as the researcher's own observation without disclosure

---

## Publication ethics

Before any paper or portfolio artifact is published:
- All evidence sources must be reviewable
- No claim may appear in a paper that is not traceable to evidence
- Co-authors (if any) must review the ethics and boundaries document
- The limitations section of any paper must state the single-participant scope
