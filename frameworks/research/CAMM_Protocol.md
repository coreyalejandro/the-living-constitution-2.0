## Purpose

CAMM is the Tier-1 research design protocol for all studies conducted under TLC
governance. It defines how quantitative and qualitative methods are combined
under constitutional oversight, with neurodivergent-first design at every stage.

CAMM applies to every TLC-governed study. It is not optional.

---

## Design Pattern: Concurrent Embedded

```
Quantitative Strand (MSEM / Bayesian)
        ↕ (embedded, continuous exchange)
Qualitative Strand (narrative, participatory)
        ↓
Constitutional Oversight Layer
        ↓
Neurodivergent Governance (NAB / CHAE)
        ↓
Tier-1 Validation Loop
```

---

## Quantitative Requirements (Full Scale)

- Minimum N: 1,000 (4 sites, 250 per site)
- Model: Multilevel Structural Equation Modeling (MSEM)
  - Level 1: individual participants
  - Level 2: site / cultural context
  - Cross-level interactions: cognitive profile × constitutional compliance
- Sensitivity analysis: Bayesian (flat priors + informative priors)
- Power: > 0.95 (updated from bootstrapped pilot effect sizes before preregistration amendment)
- Pre-registration: OSF locked analysis plan before data collection
- Multi-group invariance testing: configural → metric → scalar

## Quantitative Requirements (Bootstrapped Pilot)

- Minimum N: 10 (≥3 cognitive profiles represented)
- Model: Non-parametric pre-post analysis + PLS-SEM (proof-of-concept only)
- Effect sizes: reported as input to full-scale power analysis
- Output: NOT FOR PUBLICATION — for preregistration amendment and Tier-1 Compliance Report only

---

## Qualitative Requirements

- Method: Thematic analysis (Braun & Clarke 2006) + narrative analysis (Bruner 1990)
- AI-assisted: allowed for first-pass coding; all final codes require human review
- Member checking: mandatory — participants review interpretations before publication
- Saturation: theoretical saturation required before closing qualitative strand
- Governance: all qualitative analysis plans reviewed by NAB before data collection

---

## Integration Point

Quantitative and qualitative findings must be reconciled before publication.
Contradictions must be reported, not suppressed. If quant and qual conflict,
the qualitative strand has primary interpretive weight (Narrative-First, Article VIII
of Core Constitution).
